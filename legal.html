Notice of Privacy Practices (HIPAA)

Effective: January 1, 2025 ¬∑ Updated: February 2026

This notice describes how medical information about you may be used and disclosed, and how you can get access to this information.

Our Duty to Protect Your Health Information

Joseph Psychiatry PLLC is required by law to maintain the privacy of your protected health information (PHI), to provide you with notice of our legal duties and privacy practices, and to follow the terms of this notice. We will notify you if a breach of your unsecured PHI occurs.

How We May Use and Disclose Your Health Information

  • Treatment: To provide, coordinate, or manage your healthcare and related services, including coordination with other providers.
  • Payment: To bill and receive payment for services, including insurance verification.
  • Healthcare Operations: For quality assessment, training, licensing, and accreditation.

Uses Requiring Your Authorization

We will obtain written authorization before using PHI for marketing, sale of PHI, and most uses of psychotherapy notes. You may revoke authorization at any time in writing.

Your Rights

  • Request restrictions on how your PHI is used or disclosed
  • Request confidential communications
  • Inspect and copy your health information
  • Request amendments to your health information
  • Receive an accounting of disclosures
  • Obtain a paper copy of this notice
  • File a complaint if your privacy rights have been violated

Complaints

File complaints with our practice or the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, Office for Civil Rights. We will not retaliate against you for filing a complaint.

Telehealth Informed Consent

Effective: January 1, 2025 ¬∑ Updated: February 2026

By participating in telehealth services, you acknowledge and agree to the following:

Nature of Telehealth

Telehealth uses secure, HIPAA-compliant audio and video technology to deliver healthcare remotely. It is not identical to in-person care and has inherent limitations.

Benefits

  • Improved access from anywhere in New York State
  • Reduced travel time and costs
  • Convenient evening hours
  • Care continuity when in-person isn't feasible

Risks and Limitations

  • Technology failures may interrupt sessions
  • Small risk of unauthorized access despite encryption
  • Physical examination cannot be performed remotely
  • May not be appropriate for all clinical situations

Your Responsibilities

Ensure a private, quiet location; use a device with camera, microphone, and stable internet; be in New York State during appointments; notify provider of technical issues.

Emergency Protocols

Telehealth is not for emergencies. Call 911 or go to the ER for psychiatric emergencies. Contact the 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline by calling or texting 988.

Consent

By scheduling a telehealth appointment, you consent to receiving services via telehealth. You may withdraw consent at any time in writing.
